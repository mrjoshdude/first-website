What is extinction?

Extinction is the death of a species. It is the ending of a type of creature to such a degree that it can only ever been seen again in books. Extinction is sad, but for millions of years, it has also been perfectly natural. It is the way of the world for things to change, new things to be born, and old things to die or change. But the past few hundred years have been different.

The footprint that the human being has left on this Earth has changed everything. We have taken this planet as our own with no regard for the other living things that shared it with us. We have warped and changed things to suit our needs and destroyed the lives and homes of many of the wonderful and brilliant plants and animals living here with us.

Current rates of extinction are estimated to be between 100 and 1,000 times higher than the background rate. This is anything but natural. We are living in a mass extinction event of our own creation, and only we can pull ourselves back out. We have lost the woolly mammoths and the dodos forever. The tiger still lives, the elephant still walks the Earth. But for how much longer depends on us.

What is at stake?

Imagine living in a world where the rhinoceros is taught to children in the same light as the sabre-toothed cat. As a creature that once roamed the Earth but no longer does. Imagine telling your grandchildren about the panda, because you know they will never see one for themselves.

So many of the most iconic and well-loved animals in the world are teetering on the edge of extinction, of being gone forever. We may live in the end times for the snow leopard or the orangutan and so many people don't even realise it.
